evangelion 01.17.2026 I recently finished Neon Genesis Evangelion and End of Evangelion, and I found it to be rather profound. To portray a diverse ranges of emotions, loneliness, sadness, depression, isolation, so raw. I think that I was able to find a bit of myself in each of the characters, which made watching it all the more enjoyable. I would highly recommend it to anyone who likes introspective and psychological animes, and also mechs.


limbo 01.17.2026 i tend to think above love and relationships alot. the other day i was worried that i would never find someone like me due to how atypical i am compared to those that are my age. i feel like mentally i am 30 while others my age are still mentally stuck in their teens. people of a high maturity that are my age are hard to come by, so i often tell myself to wait for God's timing, though that does stop the feeling of being behind.


panopticon 01.18.2026 my sister told me about panopticons and how they are basically prisons where the prisoners self regulate themselves, there is a watchtower in the center of the prison that has a view into everyones cell. the catch is that there is no one in the tower, but the prisons don't know that so they act as if they are being watched at all times. i say this because i feel like a majority of people (myself included) live in such states, where we are governed by what we think others see or think of us doing. i personally feel like everytime that i am outside that i am being watched and scrutinzed, having to constantly remind myself that i am not the center of attention, and that in reality, people are so worried about their own watchtowers that they are not in my watchtower.


art and personality 01.19.2026 though this is rather obvious, i think that art and its expression is the closest we could ever hope to get to fully know someone. if you really think about it, art is how we explain what we are thinking and feeling. through sades music, which is her art, we can see how she views love through her own life experiences, whether that be pain or heartbreak. you, the reader can view my heart, mind and soul through my art (this website and the entries on it). you could even see how someone is without them being their, through how they express themselves through their belongings. i can tell that my friend josh really likes cars through not only out verbal conversations, but also the back of his laptop, which is covered with racing and car related stickers. i think that if someone truly wants to understand you in your totality, they will also understand you art and expression.
human instrumentality project after finshing evangelion, i started to think more and more about the human instrumentiality project and the ethics behind it. was it truly a ethical decision to make? on one hand, its not ethical and it overrides everyone personal freedoms and liberties, forcing them to lose their individuality for the sake of the whole. on the other hand, it seems like a worthwhile price, as this essentially eliminates core feelings that everyone has felt and dealt with, loneliness, isolation, depression, etc. i think people would originally say that they wouldn't go for it as it violates other human rights, but if you give it some thought, it would be a tempting offer. i also think it speaks about the lengths people will go to soothe their own pain and suffering. i personally can't fathom wiping out individuality as a whole now, but i'm sure a desperate version of myself would think on the offer. so i pose a to you the reader, to what lengths should we eliminate humans rights to ease the pain and suffering of ourselves and others.
